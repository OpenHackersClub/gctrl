## Thesis

The Feb 28 2026 US–Israeli strike on Iran ([[2026-04-18--wikipedia-2026-iran-war]]) decisively degraded Iran's retaliatory missile + proxy capabilities without resolving the core dispute: enrichment. Khamenei's assassination and Mojtaba Khamenei's succession produced a regime that is weaker militarily, stronger in grievance, and opaque to [[iaea]] after Iran terminated access Feb 28. The April 8 US–Iran ceasefire and April 16 Israel–Lebanon truce are tactical pauses, not strategic resolution.

The investable framing: **the military phase is likely over for ~3–6 months; the nuclear-ambiguity phase is just starting**. In that window, oil risk fades (blockade will lift in negotiations), but defense + geopolitical hedging doesn't — because a second strike becomes the base case if IAEA never regains access.

## Key questions

1. Will the April 22 deadline produce a framework deal, a deadline extension, or strike resumption? — base case: extension with face-saving language; strike is tail risk.
2. How much of Iran's 440.9 kg 60%-enriched stockpile survived Feb–Mar strikes? — DNI says no resumption, IAEA can't verify ([[2026-04-18--armscontrol-iran-nuclear-status]]).
3. Are [[hezbollah]] and [[houthis]] permanently degraded, or reconstituting? — Belfer says structural collapse ([[2026-04-18--belfer-proxy-degradation]]); Houthi Mar 28 resumption is counter-evidence.
4. Does Strait of Hormuz shipping normalize post-blockade, or stay bid at a permanent risk premium?
