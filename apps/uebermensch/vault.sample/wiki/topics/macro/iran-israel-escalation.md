# Iran–Israel 2025–2026 escalation

Tracks the combined US–Israeli military campaign against [[iran]] that began Feb 28 2026 with Operation Epic Fury (US) and Operation Roaring Lion (Israel). The operation opened with the assassination of [[ali-khamenei]] and coordinated strikes on [[natanz]], [[fordow]], and Iranian missile infrastructure ([[2026-04-18--wikipedia-2026-iran-war]]).

## Timeline anchors

- **Feb 28 2026** — Combined strikes begin. [[ali-khamenei]] killed. [[iaea]] access terminated by Iran.
- **Mar 1 2026** — Mojtaba Khamenei announced as successor Supreme Leader.
- **Mar 28 2026** — [[houthis]] resume Red Sea attacks.
- **Apr 6 2026** — Iran rejects US ceasefire terms; Maj Gen Majid Khademi (IRGC intelligence) killed in joint US–Israeli strike.
- **Apr 8 2026** — First US–Iran ceasefire reached via Pakistan mediation (Asim Munir).
- **Apr 13 2026** — [[donald-trump]] announces US naval blockade of [[strait-of-hormuz]].
- **Apr 16 2026** — Israel–Lebanon 10-day truce begins.
- **Apr 22 2026** — Two-week ceasefire deadline; fate of framework deal tested.

## Active state as of [[2026-04-18--wikipedia-2026-iran-war|2026-04-18]]

- US–Iran ceasefire holding at brittle level; blockade of [[strait-of-hormuz]] still in force.
- Israel–Lebanon truce holding; [[hezbollah]] assessed as structurally collapsed ([[2026-04-18--belfer-proxy-degradation]]).
- [[iaea]] reports no verification access since Feb 28 ([[2026-04-18--armscontrol-iran-nuclear-status]]).
- Economic damage: Iran ~$270B, Arab states ~$120B, oil markets in largest disruption since 1970s.
