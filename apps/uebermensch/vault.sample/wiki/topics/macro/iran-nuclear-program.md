# Iran nuclear program

Iran's uranium-enrichment program, centered at [[natanz]] and [[fordow]], is the strategic root cause of the 2026 war. By Feb 2026 Iran had accumulated 440.9 kg of 60%-enriched UF6 and 184.1 kg of 20%-enriched UF6 — short-notice breakout capacity estimated at 5–6 weapons in under two weeks if weaponization decisions were taken.

## Facility status

- [[natanz]] — primary enrichment site. Struck in opening phase. Status post-strike unknown to [[iaea]].
- [[fordow]] — hardened mountain facility. Announced late-2024 plan to scale 60% UF6 production from 4.7 → 37 kg/month.
- Isfahan — conversion + uranium metallurgy. Struck.
- Arak — heavy-water reactor; plutonium pathway historically blocked by IAEA monitoring.

## Access status

Iran terminated the Cairo agreement on Nov 20 2025 and cut [[iaea]] access entirely on Feb 28 2026 — **the most significant verification blackout in IAEA–Iran history** ([[2026-04-18--armscontrol-iran-nuclear-status]]). DNI Tulsi Gabbard testified Mar 18 2026 that Iran has not resumed enrichment; IAEA cannot confirm.

## Breakout timeline

Pre-JCPOA ≈ 12 months. Late 2024 ≈ under 2 weeks for 5–6 weapons-worth. Post-strike: unknown — depends on how much stockpile + centrifuge capacity survived.
