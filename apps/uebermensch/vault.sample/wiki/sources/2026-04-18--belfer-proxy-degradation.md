# Belfer Center — Degradation of Iran's Proxy Model

## Thesis

A fundamental collapse of [[iran]]'s regional proxy architecture — from coordinated "axis of resistance" to fragmented, survivalist actors prioritising self-preservation over Tehran's strategic objectives.

## [[hezbollah]] — terminal decline

- Post-8 Oct 2023 intervention: [[israel]] conducted **9,812 strikes** inside Lebanon during 2023–24.
- Hezbollah fired ~**15,400 rockets**; only ~18% at non-evacuated areas; ~90% of deeper strikes intercepted.
- Medium-range missiles reduced to ~1/5 of previous levels; mainly short-range arsenal remains.
- Hassan Nasrallah assassinated **Sep 2024**; IRGC forced into greater operational involvement.
- **Jan 2025** — Lebanon elected first president without Iranian/Syrian imposition; constitutional amendments affirmed state monopoly on arms.
- World Bank reconstruction estimate: **$11B** — far exceeding combined Iran–Hezbollah capacity.
- **Mar 2026** Operation Epic Fury intervention proved strategically futile, accelerating collapse.

## [[houthis]] — strategic retrenchment

- US Operation Rough Rider **2025**: ~1,100 airstrikes.
- [[israel]] killed Houthi cabinet members Aug 2025.
- [[israel]] strikes on Hodeida Port fuel storage cost ~$20M direct losses.
- US sanctions designations Dec 2023 (renewed 2025); FTO designation Mar 2025.
- Abdul Malik al-Houthi withdrew from public visibility late 2025.
- By early 2026, abandoned "axis of resistance" rhetoric → "secondary supporter" framing.
- Regional power projection effectively ceased — but remains the most resilient of Iran's proxies.

## Iraqi Popular Mobilization Forces — fragmentation

- 2020 assassinations of Qasem Soleimani + Abu Mahdi Muhandis removed organizational cohesion.
- Parliamentary seats: 48 (2018) → 17 (2021).
- Assad regime collapse **Dec 2024** accelerated external pressure.
- During June 2025 Iran–Israel conflict + Operation Epic Fury (Feb 2026), only Islamic Resistance in Iraq factions engaged; most PMFs remained inactive.

## Strategic implications

The degradation reflects [[iran]]'s **strategic overextension**, not tactical adaptation. The region is transitioning from militia-dominated hybrid security architecture toward state-centric order. Opportunities for Lebanon, Yemen, Iraq, and Syria to rebuild state institutions — contingent on sustained US-allied support and inclusive political processes.
