# Wikipedia — 2026 Iran war

## Summary

On **28 February 2026**, the United States and [[israel]] launched surprise airstrikes against [[iran]] during ongoing US–Iran negotiations. Trump-administration justifications cited forestalling Iranian retaliation, destroying missile capabilities, preventing nuclear weapons development, and achieving regime change.

The conflict began with coordinated strikes targeting military installations, government facilities, and infrastructure. The most significant early casualty was [[ali-khamenei]]'s assassination during the initial strikes.

Iran responded with hundreds of drones and ballistic missiles directed at Israel and American bases across the Gulf region — including facilities in Bahrain, Jordan, Kuwait, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, and the UAE. By **8 April**, the first ceasefire agreement emerged through Pakistani mediation (Asim Munir), though fragile.

The conflict escalated further with Israeli attacks in Lebanon on **8 April**, prompting Iran to refuse reopening the [[strait-of-hormuz]]. On **13 April**, [[donald-trump]] announced a naval blockade of Iran. A separate 10-day truce between Israel and Lebanon began on **16 April**.

## Key figures

- [[donald-trump]] led the American campaign, supported by Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth.
- Israeli leadership: PM [[benjamin-netanyahu]] and FM Israel Katz.
- Iranian: [[ali-khamenei]] killed in initial strikes; Mojtaba Khamenei succeeded as Supreme Leader. Other casualties include military commanders Abdolrahim Mousavi and Aziz Nasirzadeh; official Ali Larijani.

## Territorial scope

War extends across West Asia and the Indian Ocean. Targets include Iranian military bases and nuclear facilities, Israeli positions, and US installations throughout the Persian Gulf. Iran's counterattacks reached multiple Arab nations; drone strikes on Cyprus's Akrotiri base, interceptions over Turkey. [[strait-of-hormuz]] became a critical flashpoint after Iran closed it.

## Current status (mid-April 2026)

Conflict remains under temporary ceasefires rather than conclusive resolution. The Iran–US ceasefire began 8 April; Israel–Hezbollah/Lebanon arrangement 16 April. Fundamental disagreements persist regarding [[strait-of-hormuz]] reopening and broader settlement terms. Economic damage: Iran >$270B, Arab countries >$120B; global oil markets face unprecedented disruption since the 1970s energy crisis.
