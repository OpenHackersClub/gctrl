# Arms Control Association — Iran nuclear program status

## Enrichment stockpile (Nov 2024 assessment)

- **182 kg** enriched to **60%** U-235 — approaching weapons-grade, no civilian use.
- **840 kg** enriched to **20%**.
- **2,595 kg** enriched to **5%**.

(IAEA Mar 2026 board report raised the 60%-U-235 figure to 440.9 kg UF6 and 20% to 184.1 kg UF6 as of June 2025.)

Dramatic expansion since the May 2018 US withdrawal from the JCPOA.

## Centrifuge capacity at [[natanz]] and [[fordow]]

- 42 cascades of IR-1 centrifuges.
- 37 IR-2 cascades (15 operating).
- 13 operating IR-4 cascades.
- 15 IR-6 cascades (7 functional).

Late-2024 plan: install 32 additional cascades; scale [[fordow]] 60%-UF6 production from 4.7 → 37 kg/month.

## Breakout timeline

As of late 2024, Iran could produce enough weapons-grade uranium for **5–6 bombs in under two weeks**, compared to ~12 months under JCPOA. The [[fordow]] expansion would further accelerate this.

## [[iaea]] monitoring constraints

- Iran suspended Additional Protocol implementation Feb 2021.
- Halted daily inspections, continuous surveillance, Modified Code 3.1 submissions.
- The letter of **20 November 2025** informed the DG that the Cairo agreement was "no longer valid and shall henceforth be regarded as terminated".
- **Iran terminated all IAEA access on 28 February 2026.**
- Since the strikes, the Agency has not been provided with access to any of these facilities and cannot assess their current status.

## Weaponization

Iran's organized nuclear weapons program concluded in 2003 per US intelligence assessments. The US IC warned Nov 2024 that Iran's current activities "better position it to produce" nuclear weapons "if it so chooses." Senior Iranian officials have suggested potential reconsideration of the Supreme Leader's prohibition on nuclear weapons if security threats materialize. Iranian atomic energy officials acknowledge possessing "the necessary technical capabilities to weaponize," though current weaponization development remains unconfirmed.

## Post-strike assessment

DNI Tulsi Gabbard testified on **18 March 2026** that Iran has not resumed enriching uranium. A confidential [[iaea]] report said it has not been able to confirm if Iran has suspended its nuclear enrichment program since the strikes. The current situation represents **the most significant verification blackout in IAEA–Iran history**.
